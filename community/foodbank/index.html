Honiton Foodbank.

A local store of food, run by volunteers from churches and the wider community, for anyone in Honiton facing hard times.

What we do

Nobody in our town should go hungry.

The foodbank is a local store of non-perishable food, operated by volunteers from churches and the wider community in and around Honiton. It's there for anyone experiencing financial difficulty.

If you're struggling to buy food and you live in Honiton or one of the surrounding villages, you no longer need an appointment — just come down during our opening hours and we'll provide you with food.

When we’re open

Tuesday 12:30 – 3:00pm
Friday 12:30 – 3:00pm

Find us at The King’s Centre, Lee’s Building, High Street, Honiton, EX14 1DH. We only deliver in exceptional circumstances — please call to discuss.

📧 foodbank@thecommunitychurch.co.uk

  • MIND, Devon 01752 512280
  • St Paul’s Church, Honiton 01404 44035
  • Honiton Town Council 01404 42957
  • East Devon District Council 01404 515616
  • Honiton JobCentre Plus 0800 1690190
  • Citizens Advice, East Devon 01395 265070

How to help

Donate food, or give.

Without donations, there's no food and no foodbank. You can drop non-perishable items at any of our collection points around Honiton, or give financially so we can buy what we're short of.
